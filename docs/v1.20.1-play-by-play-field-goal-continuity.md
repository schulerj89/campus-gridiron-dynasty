# v1.20.1 Play-by-Play Field Goal Continuity

## Expert Finding

The play-by-play builder allowed any queued scoring event to trigger once a drive crossed a random scoring threshold. For field goals, that meant a long early-down conversion could move the ball into range and immediately display the next play as a fourth-down kick, even though no second- or third-down plays were logged.

The same drive model also capped field position at the opponent 1-yard line while still forcing normal plays to gain at least one yard. That allowed repeated `1st & 1` gains from the 1 with no touchdown. If the snap guard then ended the drive, possession could flip without a terminal punt, score, interception, or turnover on downs.

## Fix

- Field goal and missed field goal events now wait until the drive reaches fourth down.
- Normal non-touchdown plays now use a shared max-gain cap and cannot cross the goal line.
- The drive state transition defensively caps positive progress before deciding whether a new first down was earned.
- If a scripted drive runs out of normal attempts or hits its snap guard, the play-by-play now appends a terminal sequence: queued score, fourth-down field goal, turnover on downs in scoring territory, or punt.
- Regression coverage scans deterministic games for field-goal jumps, positive non-touchdown gains from the opponent 1, and possession changes after non-terminal plays.

## Follow-up Direction

A full rewrite is not required for this patch, but the next simulation refactor should move toward a reducer-style `Drive` object that owns field state, remaining attempts, queued scoring event, play list, and terminal reason. Possession should only flip after that object reports a terminal result.
